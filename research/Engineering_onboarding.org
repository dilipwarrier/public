* Introduction

This onboarding document maps the software ecosystem by tracing
the development lifecycle through the V-model. The goal is to identify
friction points, toolchain gaps, and areas where modern engineering
practices can accelerate delivery.

* Phase 1: The Left Side (Decomposition & Definition)

Your goal here is to understand how product intent becomes software
architecture.

** Requirements Analysis
- How are system requirements formally managed and communicated?
- Do we require strict traceability between a product requirement and a
  specific line of code?
- How do we resolve conflicting requirements between the hardware and
  software teams?
- What tools are we using in this phase to manage requirements and
  traceability?

** System & Architecture Design
- What is our overarching hardware-software partitioning strategy?
- How is the operating system layer defined across our product lines?
- How are hardware interfaces documented and handed off from electrical
  engineering to software?
- What tools are we using in this phase for system modeling and
  architecture documentation?

** Module Design
- Are we leveraging model-based systems engineering or code generation
  for complex logic?
- How do we allocate and track memory and timing budgets for individual
  software modules?
- What tools are we using in this phase for module design and resource
  tracking?

* Phase 2: The Point of the V (Implementation & Build)

Your goal is to understand the daily friction of the developer experience.

** Toolchain & Environment
- How difficult is it for a new developer to set up a local build
  environment?
- What specific cross-compiler toolchains are required for our targets?
- How long does a clean build take, and what is the primary build system?
- What tools are we using in this phase to manage dependencies and
  execute builds?

** Developer Workflow & AI
- What does the standard local development environment look like for our
  engineers?
- What version control and branching strategy governs our daily commits?
- How are developers currently utilizing AI assistants, and what are our
  corporate IP boundaries?
- What tools are we using in this phase for code authoring, version
  control, and AI assistance?

* Phase 3: The Verification Layer (Inspection & Review)

Your goal is to understand how the team ensures code quality and
conformance before a single test is dynamically executed.

** Code Review Culture & Mechanics
- What is the mechanical process for initiating and conducting a code
  review?
- Do we have established norms for the size and turnaround time of a
  pull request?
- Are our reviews purely manual, or do we leverage automated checkers
  prior to human inspection?
- How do we handle debates over architectural decisions within the
  review process?
- What tools are we using in this phase to facilitate and document
  code reviews?

** Static Analysis & Compliance
- How do we automate static analysis within our development pipeline?
- Do we require strict adherence to embedded safety standards, and how
  is that compliance enforced?
- What is our team's stance on compiler warnings and acceptable
  technical debt?
- What tools are we using in this phase for static analysis and
  standards compliance?

** Design Inspection
- Do we require a formal design review process before implementation
  begins?
- How do we verify that the hardware-software interface documents
  match the actual code?
- What tools are we using in this phase to inspect and verify
  upfront design?

* Phase 4: The Right Side (Integration & Validation)

Your goal is to understand how the team proves the software actually works
when it meets physical reality.

** Unit Testing (Validates Module Design)

- How do we isolate and test individual components of our logic?
- Can developers easily execute the entire unit test suite locally?
- What is our strategy for mocking hardware interactions during unit
  tests?
- Do we run tests on the host architecture or strictly on the target
  hardware?
- What tools are we using in this phase to author and execute unit tests?

** Integration Testing (Validates Architecture)

- How do we validate the boundary between our software and the physical
  electrical hardware?
- Have we invested in an automated Hardware-in-the-Loop setup?
- Do developers have sufficient access to prototype hardware, or is
  contention a bottleneck?
- What tools are we using in this phase for integration and HITL testing?

** System Testing & Release (Validates Requirements)

- Are validation tests automatically triggered as gating checks in our
  CI/CD pipeline?
- How do we securely package and cryptographically sign the final
  firmware payload?
- What is our primary deployment mechanism for reaching devices in the
  field?
- If an update fails, what is the automated rollback mechanism to
  prevent bricking the device?
- What tools are we using in this phase for continuous integration,
  release management, and deployment?

* Phase 5: The Delivery Engine (Agile & Team Operations)

Your goal here is to understand how the engineering organization structures
its humans and time to execute the technical V-model.

** Team Topology & Sizing

- Are we organized as cross-functional feature teams or specialized
  component teams (e.g., drivers vs. application logic)?
- What is the average size of a pod or scrum team, and what is the
  ratio of developers to QA/test engineers?
- How do we handle dependencies between embedded software teams and
  the electrical/hardware engineering teams?

** Agile Rhythms & Hardware Reality

- What is the duration of our standard sprint, and how strictly is
  that timebox enforced?
- How do our agile software sprints synchronize with the inflexible
  realities of hardware revision cycles (board spins)?
- Do we run formal planning, grooming, and retrospective ceremonies,
  or do we operate on a more continuous Kanban flow?
- How do we handle interrupt-driven work (e.g., manufacturing line
  stoppages or critical field bugs) during a sprint?

** The Tooling Question

- What tools are we using in this phase to manage backlogs, track
  sprint velocity, and document team decisions?

* Phase 6: The Human Element (People & Performance)

Your goal here is to understand how the organization evaluates, rewards,
and communicates with its engineers.

** Performance & Growth

- How are performance reviews formally conducted, and what is the
  evaluation cadence?
- Do we have a codified engineering leveling guide or career ladder
  for individual contributors?
- How are performance goals established, and how do they tie into
  company-wide objectives?
- What tools are we using in this phase for performance management
  and goal tracking?

** Communication & Cadence

- What is the cadence, structure, and expectation for department
  all-hands and team staff meetings?
- How is critical top-down business information routinely disseminated
  to the engineering floor?
- What tools are we using in this phase to facilitate asynchronous
  team communication and announcements?

** Organization & Compensation

- What is the current distribution of titles (e.g., Junior, Senior,
  Staff, Principal) across the engineering team?
- How is compensation structured and reviewed (e.g., annual merit
  cycles, equity grants, bonus targets)?
- Who are the key individuals holding critical domain knowledge or
  acting as single points of failure?
- What HRIS or management tools are we using in this phase to track
  organizational charts and compensation bands?
